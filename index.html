    Message From CEO

    We founded EK PEHEL ANANT TAK FOUNDATION with the mission to use urban discard as a tool to alleviate poverty and raise the dignity of people living in poverty in the country.

    • Individuals and governments alike are overwhelmed by the emerging complexities of social problems. We need to help everyone from a rural woman to a malnourished child with all their basic needs, from health care to food, water, hygiene to educational support, and to help them embrace new technology.
    • Poor people must be able to develop their own solutions while being rewarded with urban materials for tackling basic yet neglected issues and joining this foundation. It is an ever-growing movement to come together and work together to achieve a society that is mutually supported.
    • Let us walk on this path together and let us give them "Acche Din" for real by remembering the saying "Vasudhaiva Kutumbakam" together.

    Our Guiding Principles

    Our Passion

    We believe in creating difference in the lives of people in need. Creating "Passion for Learning". Giving a respectful life to "Not so Privileged People" with complete Gratitude of what we have.



    Our Mission

    Our mission is to create the largest human support chain in India, open 1000 education, health and food centres by 2030 across the country. To work towards creating a better India which provides basic health, education and empowerment to every citizen. To be a Knowledge/ Execution Partner of Schools, NPOs, Corporates, and Government agencies for development programmes across India.



    Our Vision

    Our vision is to built a community where no one dies due to shortage of health related facilities, a world where everyone gets quality education without worrying about expenses, a healthy, happy, secure, and a respectful world.



    Our Values

    We are an NPO, and Proud of that. We believe in transparency and work towards building trust, togetherness and collaboration. We give our 100%.

    Some Of Our Essential Missions

    VIDYA HAI HAQ

    It’s time to reconsider education.

    Today, 32 million Indian children of age up to 13 years have never attended any school, the majority of them belonging to the socially disadvantaged class.

      So how we can provide education to maximum children living in rural / urban areas in less amount and by proving facilities similar to that of private school?

    • We can’t control the increasing population. Fortunately, we do not have to—we have to open school in each and every rural/urban area which will not only provide quality education but also employment to many educated unemployed people around the country
      Contribute

    NEER

    Water supports not just the needs of humans, but also that of crops and animals. However, the availability of water in a region by itself does not make it viable for consumption unless it is treated and made safe for drinking.

      Its access and usage depend upon several factors such as the installation of water purification systems, presence of water taps in households and functional water pumps. For conserving water and preventing it from contamination, communities need to be aware of safe and desired WASH habits, including the use of toilets.

    • In order to address the challenges of water scarcity and water contamination, and to contain the risks related to improper sanitation, our NPO has started working to implement the following initiatives under their Integrated Drinking Water and Sanitation Programme, while deploying innovative and affordable technology.
      • Contribute

    BADLAAV

    Increasing mobile penetration by a mere 10 per cent will increase per capital GDP in developing countries by 0.81 per cent. Growing mobile phone penetration in rural regions will lead to the next wave of growth in the adoption of digital technology.

      The opportunity is ripe for designing early-level interventions to help rural communities discover the power of the digital medium and the internet in a structured and guided manner. However, in order to achieve this, traditional societal barriers that women face, concerning exposure to technology and handling of gadgets, have to be overcomed

    • Under the goal of digital literacy, we provide guided online experiences that help users discover and mine information that is most useful and relevant to their life – information that will eventually decrease the current information gap faced in the region.
      • Contribute

    PRASTHAN

    The initiatives under the migration portfolio are aimed at helping migrants face the challenges of migration.

      This is achieved by linking them to various social security and financial services, and health and sanitation facilities, which in turn eases the stress they face due to residential, occupational and social vulnerabilities.

    • Our vision is to support the development of migrant communities by introducing sustainable and scalable solutions to achieve large-scale impact
      • Contribute

    SATYA

    A robust and responsive justice delivery system is an imperative for a country’s development. When justice is delayed or not served, victims are left with little hope that things will change. Changes within the justice system are complicated, systemic and interlinked and require critical attention and interventions.

      We would be spearheading social change in the criminal justice and juvenile justice systems in India by focusing on addressing the needs of the most marginalised sections of society.

    • We work to bring about social change in some of the most vulnerable sections of community and social justice is a key aspect of this. The trend of increasing violence against women and children is a major concern.
      • Contribute

    SANSADHAN

      The energy programmes are designed with the objective to:

    • Address the nation’s challenge of providing power on demand to the most underserved communities, and satisfy the energy needs of the rural community.
    • Provide rural households with clean cooking energy to overcome health and environmental hazards caused by using bio-fuels.
    • Empower rural entrepreneurs for providing last-mile energy services, besides skilling them to create needs.
    • Conduct extensive research to develop technologies for affordable and sustainable systems, which increase income and reduce drudgery.
      • Contribute

    JEEVIKA

    Agriculture is the backbone of the Indian economy as 70% of India’s population depends directly or indirectly on agriculture for livelihood. According to the World Bank, a focus on three key areas will impact the country’s overall development and the improved welfare of its rural poor

    • Raise agricultural productivity per unit of land through crop diversification.
    • Reduce rural poverty through policies that benefit tribal communities, poor and marginalised communities, women and scheduled castes
    • Ensure food security through a productive, diversified agricultural sector
      • Contribute

    ABHIVYAKTI

    Our NPO runs 'Abhivyakti' - a skill development initiative exclusively for the underprivileged people across the city. This program provide trainees with relevant vocational skills to enable them earn sustainable livelihood.

    • Through this, we have trained over 50+ candidates free of cost and empowered them to build a better future for the nation and themselves.
    • The aim of this scheme is to improve educational and socio-economic conditions of the target group with a view to upgrade their skillset to enable them to start income generation activites on their own or get gainfully employed in some sector or the other.
    • Ensure food security through a productive, diversified agricultural sector
      • Contribute

    ANN

    Despite a remarkable progress in the economy, the country has failed to eradicate hunger at the grassroot level and is home to 15% of the world’s hunger.

    • World produces enough food to feed everyone sufficiently but poverty forces many of them to sleep with empty stomach.
    • Our project “ANN” is an initiative that works in this direction. We have been trying to provide meals to homeless people in the city.
    • Our NPO collects unconsumed food from restaurants, canteens, banquet halls and packs it to distribute among the hungry.
    • Our project’s aim is to improve health, lessen inequalities and end hunger sustainability through hyperlocal distribution of food.
      • Contribute

    KAYANTARAN

    Our project's vision is to enable the most impoverished rural communities in India to achieve their full potential by ensuring them access to the opportunities available to everyone else.

    • We want change that is self-driven, sustainable and impactful so as to help in transforming rural India.
    • We will be creating local and scalable solutions for rural communities that foster dignity and prosperity in villages. A community-driven sustainable model of empowerment for rural India.
    • The goal is to bring about a change in the lives of the most deprived in rural communities, in the poorest 500 villages that we call ‘Stranded India.’
      • Contribute

    HIMMAT

    Violence against women and girls affects 1 in 3 women globally. Women living in informal urban settlements are particularly vulnerable to violence.

    • Women and girls face physical, sexual and emotional violence in both private and public spaces. Our approach embodies gender-transformative change, rights and women-centred intervention.
    • We work to empower communities to support individuals in cases of violence.
    • Our community-based women volunteers monitor the safety of women and children in their area, provide emotional support and connect women to crisis intervention services. We will advocate for public policies that can enhance institutional response to domestic violence.
      • Contribute

    SANSTHAAN

    Financial Inclusion (FI) is the pursuit of making financial services accessible at affordable costs and it strives to address constraints that exclude people from participating in the financial sector. The core objectives of financial inclusion initiatives are:

    • To make formal sector credit accessible to the beneficiaries in a fair and transparent way
    • Promoting community led models for insuring against unforeseen risks
    • Ensuring financial awareness and education of the communities.
      • Contribute
